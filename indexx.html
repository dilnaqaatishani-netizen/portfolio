Questions people actually ask

What's a good typing speed?

Most people type 35–45 WPM casually. 60+ WPM is considered fast for office work, and 80+ puts you in professional-typist territory. Track your own baseline first — the goal is your improvement, not a universal number.

Why does accuracy matter more than speed early on?

If you train fast with mistakes, you're memorizing the mistakes. Slowing down until your fingers consistently hit the right keys builds the muscle memory that speed later relies on.

Does this save my results?

Your best WPM and recent runs are stored only in your browser's local storage. Nothing is sent to a server, and clearing your browser data will reset it.

Can I use this on mobile?

The page works on mobile, but typing tests are built around physical keyboards — you'll get far more useful practice on a laptop or desktop.
